Sunbay.io Investment Memo

S16VC | October 2025

PASS Investment Decision

Pass on current terms. The path to a venture-scale return is unachievable due to a 2x overvaluation ($6M post vs. $3M benchmark) at this stage. Traction is bottom-quartile ($1.5k MRR) with clear churn signals (March peak followed by April drop) and a dependency on non-scalable services revenue. Crucially, the incomplete team (no full-time CTO) and slow execution of the core AI differentiator are disqualifying for a high-velocity investment.

Key Arguments & Risks

Investment Thesis

  • Real pain: Manual AR collections consume 10-20 hrs/week for 50-200 FTE companies.
  • Founder Expertise: 14 years combined in ops/finance provides strong customer insight.
  • Revenue Proven: Total revenue of $4.5k/month shows initial willingness to pay.
  • Platform Leverage: A generalist core allows for efficient future expansion into related finance/ops verticals.

Critical Risks

  • Valuation Mismatch: $6M post valuation is 2x over market for $18k ARR ($333x ARR multiple vs. 20-50x typical).
  • Incomplete Team: CTO "joining soon" is disqualifying. Technical execution and product velocity are unproven.
  • Churn Signal: March revenue peak followed by an April drop strongly suggests failed GTM or product retention issues.
  • Non-scalable Revenue: $3k consulting (67% of total revenue) indicates deep product gaps preventing self-serve.
üìâ Bear Case / Downside

Burns $1.2M in 15 months. Stalls at $20k MRR due to high churn. Misses the Series A bar ($2.5M ARR). Total loss of capital (0x MOIC).

1. Team

  • Critical Gap: CTO commitment is disqualifying. "Full Time since [SOON]" means technical execution is unproven, and product velocity will suffer.
  • Experience Risk: Domain knowledge is present (14 years ops/finance), but scaling experience is absent. Neither founder has scaled a company from $1M to $10M ARR.
  • Missing Roles: No design, growth, or AI specialist roles. This is a critical deficiency for an "AI voice agent" differentiator.

2. Product

  • Slow Execution: The core differentiator (AI voice agent) is still "in development" after 6 months. This suggests low product velocity or technical hurdles.
  • Value Proposition: The feature list details what it does, not why a customer switches or what specific behavior change it enables (lack of customer insight).
  • Diluted Focus: Premature expansion to three use cases (Collections, Documents, Invoicing) before dominating one specific market niche.

3. Market

  • TAM Validation: $6-9B market by 2030 (12% CAGR) validates the opportunity, but AI commoditization compresses margins for basic features.
  • Target Risk: The target segment (50-200 FTE) is price-sensitive and typically experiences 15-25% churn, which strains unit economics.
  • Bottom-Up TAM: 6M companies √ó 10% addressable √ó 3% adoption √ó $600 MRR = $108M serviceable market. Competitive but winner-take-all dynamics are not present.

4. Competition & Differentiation

  • Squeezed Middle: Competition exists at the high-end (HighRadius, $475M raised) and low-end (Zapier, Power Automate). Sunbay is positioning in the weakest "middle."
  • AI Disadvantage: AI-native startups (Gaviti, Growfin) have stronger technical teams and will likely ship better, more performant voice agents faster.
  • Differentiation Failure: The "best of both worlds" (a "Toyota Corolla" strategy) is not a 10x differentiator. Software winners need extreme performance in one dimension.

5. Traction & Benchmarking

  • Bottom Quartile: $1.5k MRR at 6 months places in the bottom 25th percentile vs. typical pre-seed benchmarks ($5k YC median). Signals weak PMF.
  • Red Flag Pattern: The March-April pattern (record count spike followed by a drop) is a severe red flag indicating a retention failure or a one-time GTM push that failed to convert.
  • Services Dependence: $3k consulting is 67% of total revenue. This manual implementation work is not scalable and requires significant product development to mitigate.

Analysis: Base returns (3.3x) are marginal for the risk profile. 278x growth is needed over 33 months at a perfect 20% MoM. Given weak traction, a bridge round is likely, increasing dilution risk and further suppressing returns.
Key Insight: $6M post is 2x market ($3-4M typical). For a 5x MOIC return on a $200k investment, the exit must be $60M (or $7.5M ARR at 8x). This requires a 417x growth multiple from the current $18k ARR, an improbable path without flawless execution.

Decision: Pass

Why pass: Returns are insufficient even in the base case (3.3x MOIC vs. 5-10x fund target). Valuation is 2x market rate ($333x ARR). Traction is bottom-quartile with significant churn signals. Team is incomplete (no full-time CTO), risking technical execution and product velocity.

Reconsider if: The company achieves $60k+ MRR with sub-10% churn. CTO is full-time with a live, proven AI voice agent. Sustained 25%+ MoM growth for 4+ months. Proper pricing occurs at a $3-4M post-money valuation in a seed extension round.
